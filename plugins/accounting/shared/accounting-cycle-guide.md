# Accounting Cycle Guide

The accounting cycle is a systematic, eight-phase process for recording, classifying, and summarizing financial transactions within an accounting period.

## Phase 1: Analyze Transactions

Review source documents (invoices, receipts, bank statements, contracts) to identify events that have a financial impact. Determine which accounts are affected and whether each account increases or decreases.

## Phase 2: Journal Entries

Record each transaction as a journal entry with equal debits and credits. Every entry must include the date, accounts affected, amounts, and a brief description. This is the book of original entry.

## Phase 3: Post to General Ledger

Transfer journal entries to the general ledger, organizing them by account. Each ledger account accumulates all debits and credits, providing a running balance. The ledger is the book of final entry.

## Phase 4: Trial Balance

List all ledger accounts and their balances. Total debits must equal total credits. If they do not, locate and correct errors before proceeding. This is an unadjusted trial balance.

## Phase 5: Adjusting Entries

Record accruals (revenue earned but not yet received, expenses incurred but not yet paid), deferrals (prepaid expenses, unearned revenue), depreciation, and other period-end adjustments to ensure revenue and expenses are recognized in the correct period.

## Phase 6: Adjusted Trial Balance

Prepare a new trial balance after posting adjusting entries. Verify that debits still equal credits. This balance serves as the basis for financial statement preparation.

## Phase 7: Financial Statements

Prepare the three core financial statements from the adjusted trial balance:
- **Income Statement** — revenues minus expenses for the period
- **Balance Sheet** — assets, liabilities, and equity at period end
- **Cash Flow Statement** — operating, investing, and financing cash flows

## Phase 8: Closing Entries

Close temporary accounts (revenues, expenses, dividends/draws) to retained earnings. After closing, only permanent accounts (assets, liabilities, equity) carry balances into the next period.

## Utility Skills

In addition to the eight phases, the following utility skills can be used at any point:

- **Variance** — Compare actual results to budget or forecast and explain differences
- **Controls** — Design internal controls to prevent errors and fraud
- **Audit** — Deep-dive review of specific accounts or transactions
- **Reconciliation** — Reconcile balances between related accounts or external statements
- **Materiality** — Determine significance thresholds for reporting and adjustment decisions
