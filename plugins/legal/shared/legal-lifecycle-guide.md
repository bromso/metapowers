# Legal Lifecycle Framework

A structured approach to legal work across five phases, from initial assessment through ongoing governance.

> **Disclaimer:** AI-generated legal content is for informational purposes only and does not constitute legal advice. All legal documents should be reviewed by a qualified attorney before use.

---

## Phase 0: Assess

**Objective:** Understand the legal landscape, audit current posture, identify risks, and map applicable regulations.

### Key Activities

- **Legal Audit** — Review existing contracts, policies, and compliance posture for gaps and exposures.
- **Risk Scoring** — Quantify legal risks using likelihood and impact assessments (see risk-matrix-template.md).
- **Regulatory Mapping** — Identify all regulations applicable to the organization's operations, products, and markets.
- **Jurisdiction Analysis** — Determine which legal jurisdictions apply and how they interact (conflicts of law, choice of law considerations).

### Outputs

- Risk register with scored and prioritized risks
- Regulatory applicability matrix
- Jurisdiction map with governing law recommendations
- Gap analysis report

---

## Phase 1: Draft

**Objective:** Create legal documents that are clear, enforceable, and appropriate for the relevant jurisdiction.

### Key Activities

- **Contracts** — Service agreements, licensing agreements, partnership agreements, vendor contracts.
- **Policies** — Privacy policies, acceptable use policies, employee handbooks, data retention policies.
- **Terms** — Terms of service, terms of use, end-user license agreements (EULAs).
- **NDAs** — Mutual and unilateral non-disclosure agreements.
- **Licensing Agreements** — Software licenses, IP licenses, content licenses.

### Key Principle

Use templates as starting points (see contract-template.md) and ensure jurisdiction-appropriate language. Boilerplate should be reviewed and customized, never blindly copied.

### Outputs

- Draft documents ready for review
- Template library with jurisdiction-specific variants
- Clause bank organized by topic and risk level

---

## Phase 2: Review

**Objective:** Analyze and negotiate legal documents with a systematic, risk-based approach.

### Key Activities

- **Contract Review** — Line-by-line analysis against organizational standards and risk tolerance.
- **Clause Analysis** — Evaluate individual clauses for enforceability, balance, and market standards.
- **Red Flag Detection** — Identify unusual, one-sided, or high-risk provisions that require negotiation or escalation.
- **IP Review** — Assess intellectual property ownership, licensing, and assignment provisions.

### Key Principle

Systematic risk-based review, not just a read-through. Every review should follow a checklist approach with clear escalation paths for identified issues.

### Review Priority Framework

1. **Critical** — Liability caps, indemnification, IP ownership, termination rights
2. **High** — Payment terms, warranties, confidentiality scope, governing law
3. **Medium** — Representations, notice provisions, assignment restrictions
4. **Low** — Formatting, defined term consistency, boilerplate ordering

### Outputs

- Marked-up documents with comments and proposed revisions
- Risk summary with recommended positions
- Negotiation playbook with fallback positions

---

## Phase 3: Comply

**Objective:** Implement and maintain compliance with applicable laws and regulations.

### Key Activities

- **GDPR/CCPA Implementation** — Privacy-by-design, consent management, data subject rights workflows.
- **Data Processing Agreements** — Standard contractual clauses, processor/controller agreements.
- **Cookie Consent** — Consent management platforms, cookie categorization, opt-in/opt-out mechanisms.
- **SOC 2 Preparation** — Policy documentation, control implementation, evidence collection.
- **Incident Response** — Breach notification procedures, response team assembly, communication templates.

### Key Principle

Compliance is ongoing, not one-time. Regulations evolve, business operations change, and compliance posture must be continuously monitored and updated.

### Compliance Cadence

| Activity | Frequency |
|---|---|
| Policy review | Annual |
| Training | Annual + onboarding |
| Risk assessment | Semi-annual |
| Control testing | Quarterly |
| Regulatory monitoring | Continuous |

### Outputs

- Implemented compliance programs with documented controls
- Training materials and completion records
- Incident response playbooks
- Compliance dashboards and reporting

---

## Phase 4: Govern

**Objective:** Build systems for ongoing legal management, not just individual documents.

### Key Activities

- **Contract Tracking** — Centralized repository with key dates, renewal triggers, and obligation tracking.
- **Compliance Monitoring** — Automated and manual checks against regulatory requirements.
- **Regulatory Change Tracking** — Monitor legislative and regulatory developments across jurisdictions.
- **Playbook Creation** — Standardized procedures for recurring legal tasks (e.g., contract negotiation, data breach response).
- **Training** — Regular legal awareness training for stakeholders across the organization.

### Key Principle

Build systems, not just documents. Effective legal governance requires processes, tools, and accountability structures that operate independently of any single individual.

### Governance Maturity Model

| Level | Description |
|---|---|
| 1 — Ad Hoc | Reactive, document-by-document approach |
| 2 — Repeatable | Templates and checklists in use |
| 3 — Defined | Documented processes with assigned owners |
| 4 — Managed | Metrics-driven with regular reporting |
| 5 — Optimized | Continuous improvement with predictive analytics |

### Outputs

- Contract management system with automated alerts
- Compliance monitoring dashboards
- Regulatory change log with impact assessments
- Legal playbooks for common scenarios
- Training program with tracked completion

---

## Phase Relationships

```
Assess → Draft → Review → Comply → Govern
  ↑                                    |
  └────────── feedback loop ───────────┘
```

Each phase feeds into the next, and governance insights cycle back to inform future assessments. Legal work is iterative — findings from any phase may trigger re-evaluation of earlier phases.
