# UX Laws & Principles

## Fitts's Law
The time to reach a target is a function of distance and size. Make important targets large and close to the user's current focus.

## Hick's Law
Decision time increases with the number and complexity of choices. Reduce options, use progressive disclosure.

## Jakob's Law
Users spend most of their time on other sites. They expect your site to work like ones they already know.

## Miller's Law
The average person can hold about 7 (± 2) items in working memory. Chunk information into digestible groups.

## Doherty Threshold
Productivity soars when response time is under 400ms. Provide instant feedback for user actions.

## Gestalt Principles
- **Proximity:** Elements close together are perceived as related
- **Similarity:** Similar elements are perceived as grouped
- **Continuity:** The eye follows smooth paths
- **Closure:** The brain completes incomplete shapes
- **Figure/Ground:** Elements are perceived as either foreground or background

## Visual Hierarchy
- Size: larger = more important
- Color: contrast draws attention
- Position: top-left (in LTR) gets attention first
- Whitespace: isolation creates emphasis
- Typography: weight and size signal importance

## Readability & Legibility
- **Legibility:** Can individual characters be distinguished? (typeface, size, contrast)
- **Readability:** Can text be easily read and understood? (line length 45-75 chars, line height 1.4-1.6, sufficient contrast)
- **Scannability:** Can users find information quickly? (headings, lists, bold keywords)

## Information Architecture
- Clear navigation hierarchy
- Consistent labeling
- Logical grouping
- Progressive disclosure for complex content

## Cognitive Load
- **Intrinsic:** Complexity of the task itself
- **Extraneous:** Unnecessary complexity from poor design
- **Germane:** Effort spent learning and building mental models
- Goal: minimize extraneous, support germane
